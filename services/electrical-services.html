Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our electrical services.

How often should I have my electrical system inspected?

We recommend having your home's electrical system professionally inspected every 3-5 years for newer homes and every 1-2 years for homes over 25 years old. Regular inspections help identify potential issues before they become serious problems and ensure your electrical system remains safe and efficient.

What are the signs that I need to upgrade my electrical panel?

Signs that indicate you may need an electrical panel upgrade include: frequently tripping circuit breakers, flickering lights, burning smells near the panel, buzzing sounds from outlets or switches, using numerous power strips or extension cords, having a panel older than 25 years, or planning significant home additions that will increase electrical demand.

Do you provide warranties for your electrical work?

Yes, all our electrical work comes with a comprehensive workmanship warranty. We stand behind the quality of our service with a 12-month guarantee on labor. Additionally, any products or parts we install carry their respective manufacturer warranties. We're committed to ensuring your complete satisfaction with our work.

How long does a typical electrical service call take?

The duration of an electrical service call varies depending on the complexity of the issue. Simple repairs or installations might take 1-2 hours, while more complex projects like rewiring or panel upgrades can take a full day or more. During our initial assessment, we'll provide you with a time estimate specific to your project.

Are your electricians licensed and insured?

Absolutely. All our electricians are fully licensed, certified, and insured. We comply with all local and national electrical codes and regulations. Our team regularly participates in continuing education to stay current with the latest industry standards, safety practices, and technological advancements.
